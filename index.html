How to get started

Here are some things you'll need to get your collection up and running

1. A Turntable (AKA Record Player)

This will most likely be the most expensive thing you have to buy when getting started and also one of the most important. Nowadays, you can get a decent turntable from anywhere from $80-$150 but we do recommend splurging a little more if you’re wanting to start a serious collection, why? A better built turntable will ensure that your records last longer, sound better and they tend to be more customizable with their parts.

2. Vinyl Records

You’re going to need something to play! This is the most fun part because you get to go out and buy all your favorite albums. (Just remember, skipping songs on a turntable isn’t as easy as pressing the “next” button.)

3. Speakers or Headphones

This is how you will listen to your music, most turntables don’t have any built-in speakers so you’ll have to buy them separately. The good thing is that you don’t necessarily have to splurge on this just yet, if you’re just getting started any speaker will do. You can also plug in your headphones to listen if you prefer!

4. Cleaning brush

Having a cleaning brush can come in handy, with this you can clean your records and by removing dust and other particles, it helps prevent unwanted noises like pops and crackles when listening. Some of these also come with a mini brush that can be used to clean the needle which is also important for a smooth experience.

5. Proper Setup and Storage

When storing your records it’s important to keep them upright, stacking your records will warp the vinyl due to the weight. Many people use crates to store vinyls but any container will do as long as the records are upright. Make sure your turntable is placed on a flat, even surface. By doing this, it allows the needle to drag itself evenly across a record’s groove. If your turntable isn’t flat it can cause damage to both the vinyl and the turntable itself.

And that's it! Happy listening
